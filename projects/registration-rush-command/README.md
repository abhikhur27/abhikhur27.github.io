# Registration Rush Commander

Decision-based static simulation about operating a university registration launch under pressure.

## Core idea

You are not tuning a chart. You are deciding what kind of failure the institution is willing to tolerate:

- slower queues
- lower perceived fairness
- weaker public trust
- higher operator burnout

Each round forces one operational tradeoff, and the ending is determined by the combined policy posture you chose.

## Why it clears the portfolio bar

- It gives the user a clear role: registration launch commander.
- Choices create different institutional outcomes, not just different visuals.
- The payoff is experiential: every action trades one failure mode against another.
